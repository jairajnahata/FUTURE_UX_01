**Design Rationale — Sri Sai Men's Hair Saloon Website**

**Why This Business :**

I picked a local hair saloon because most of them have zero 
online presence. People find out about them only through 
friends or just walking past. I thought a good website could 
actually bring them more customers and that is what I tried 
to solve with this project.


**Color Choices :**

I went with bold colors like Saffron Orange, Hot Magenta and 
Cobalt Blue on a dark background. I wanted it to feel premium 
but also fun and energetic. Orange felt right for a saloon 
because it is warm and inviting. The dark background makes 
everything pop and gives it a high end feel without being boring.


**Font Choices :**

I tried a few combinations and landed on these three.
Playfair Display for the main headings because it looks classy.
Bebas Neue for labels and section titles because it is bold and punchy.
DM Sans for the body text because it is clean and easy to read.


**Homepage :**

The first thing I thought about was what does a visitor need 
to see in the first 5 seconds. So I kept the hero section 
simple. Big headline, a short line about what the saloon 
offers and two buttons, Book Appointment and View Services. 
Nothing confusing, just clear next steps.

I added stats like 500 plus clients and 4.9 rating right 
below because people trust numbers. If someone sees that 
500 people have already visited, they feel more confident 
about walking in.


**Services Section :**

I only showed 3 services on the homepage on purpose. 
Showing everything at once can overwhelm people. The idea 
was to give them a taste and let the Services page do the 
rest. Each card has an image, price and a one line 
description, enough for someone to decide if they are 
interested.


**Why Choose Us Section :**

This part was about building trust. Instead of just listing 
features I split it into a quote on the left and 4 simple 
points on the right. The quote adds a human touch and the 
points answer the most common questions people have before 
visiting a new saloon.


**Testimonials :**

I added reviews with names and star ratings because people 
always check reviews before trying somewhere new. The 
carousel keeps it compact and the auto scroll makes the 
page feel alive without being distracting.


**Contact and Booking Page :**

I wanted booking to feel as easy as ordering food online. 
So instead of a plain form I added time slot buttons so 
people can just tap and pick. I also added a WhatsApp 
confirmation note because in India WhatsApp is how most 
people communicate and it feels more personal than an email.

The opening hours table shows every single day clearly. 
Tuesday is marked in red as a Holiday so there is no 
confusion and customers do not show up on a closed day.


**Mobile Design :**

A lot of local saloon customers are on their phones so I 
made sure everything works well on smaller screens. The 
bottom bar with Call and Book buttons stays fixed so no 
matter where you are on the page you can always take 
action in one tap.


**What I Learned :**

This project taught me how to think from a customers 
perspective rather than just making things look nice. 
Every section has a reason behind it and that is honestly 
the biggest thing I took away from this task.
